At The Hackerati, methodology is a tool for managing the common setbacks and hang-ups that would otherwise derail even the most talented engineering team. At the same time, the way we do things is a reflection of our culture: passionate, fast, and fun!

Like a lot of engineering shops, we use Scrum, so our practices should be very familiar to many. However, since so many engineering projects are challenged by poor communications, it is worthwhile to highlight several of our unique practices for fostering consistently great communications and collaboration.

The Jam Session

Jam sessions are frequent, regularly scheduled sessions to evaluate what we've done and decide what to do next. Sprint planning meetings are one form of jam session. We also use jam sessions for regular client checkpoint meetings, where we make sure that we are adding value and moving in the right direction.

The Huddle

Huddles are short, daily jam sessions focus on immediate activities and roadblocks (a.k.a daily standup or daily scrum). We huddle up at least once a day, or more often if needed.

The Wall

The wall is an online collaboration system for capturing information that the whole team can share. We use it to capture, debate, and refine ideas, plans, tasks, and decisions.

The Hangout

Hangouts are open video chat rooms that we can use to just drop in on each other, like we would if we worked in the same office.

The Launch Party

Every major milestone deserves a celebration. We call these launch parties!
